About Anna's Hummingbirds

Anna's Hummingbirds are a species of hummingbird native to the western coast of North America. They are known for their vibrant colors and unique courtship displays.

Physical Characteristics

  • Size: 3.9 to 4.3 inches (10 to 11 cm) long
  • Weight: 0.1 to 0.2 ounces (3 to 6 grams)
  • Color: Males have iridescent green feathers on their back and head, while females have gray-green feathers

Habitat and Diet

Anna's Hummingbirds can be found in a variety of habitats, including coastal areas, woodlands, and gardens. They primarily feed on nectar from flowers, but also consume insects and spiders.

Behavior

These hummingbirds are known for their territorial behavior and aggressive nature. Males perform elaborate aerial displays to attract females and defend their feeding territories.

Conservation Status

Anna's Hummingbirds are not currently considered threatened or endangered. However, habitat loss and climate change pose potential risks to their populations.
